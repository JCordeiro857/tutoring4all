Code of Conduct

Clear expectations for behaviour and interactions between tutors, students, and families.

📋 Effective: 12 January 2025  |  Next Review: 12 January 2026  |  This Code of Conduct is aligned with NSW Department of Education standards and applies to all tutors, students, and parents/guardians in both in-person and online settings.

1. Purpose

This Code of Conduct has been established to set clear expectations for behaviour and interactions between tutors, students, and parents/guardians. It applies to all Tutoring4All sessions — whether conducted in-home, at a library, or online — and to all communications outside of sessions. It is aligned with NSW Department of Education standards and relevant NSW legislation.

2. Scope

This Code applies to all tutors, students, and parents/guardians who engage with Tutoring4All, covering all in-person and online tutoring sessions as well as any communications between sessions.

3. General Principles

  • All participants must treat one another with mutual respect and dignity at all times.
  • Discrimination, harassment, and bullying of any kind are strictly prohibited.
  • Professional and appropriate boundaries must be maintained in all interactions.
  • Academic integrity and student welfare are to be prioritised at all times.

4. Tutor Responsibilities

Professional Conduct

Tutors are expected to be punctual, prepared, and organised for each tutoring session. Sessions should begin and end on time, and tutors should arrive with a clear plan for the lesson.

Dress

Tutors are expected to present neatly. Clean, casual clothing is acceptable — tutors should dress in a manner appropriate to a professional educational setting.

Confidentiality

Tutors must maintain strict confidentiality regarding any personal information about students and families obtained during the course of tutoring. Information may only be disclosed where there is a genuine risk to the safety of the student or others.

Respect & Non-Discrimination

Tutors must provide non-discriminatory services to all students regardless of background, religion, ethnicity, gender, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

Professional Boundaries

Tutors must maintain appropriate professional boundaries with students and families at all times. Intimate, inappropriate, or overly personal communication is strictly prohibited.

Academic Integrity

Tutors must promote honest, independent academic work. Completing assignments, essays, or other assessments on behalf of students is strictly prohibited. Tutors should guide and support students in developing their own skills and understanding.

5. Student Responsibilities

  • Treat tutors and other participants with respect and courtesy at all times.
  • Arrive on time and come prepared with relevant materials, textbooks, and a clear idea of what needs to be covered.
  • Complete work independently — do not request or expect tutors to complete assignments on your behalf.
  • Communicate openly about difficulties, confusion, or any personal issues that may affect learning.

6. Prohibited Behaviours

The following behaviours are strictly prohibited for all participants:

  • Harassment, bullying, or discrimination based on any protected characteristic.
  • Inappropriate physical contact or aggressive, threatening behaviour.
  • The use of alcohol, drugs, or any other illicit substances during or around tutoring sessions.
  • Sharing personal information in ways that compromise the privacy of any participant.
  • Any form of academic dishonesty, including requesting or providing assistance that constitutes cheating.

7. Conflict Resolution

Any concerns or conflicts should be addressed through the following process:

  1. Step 1: Raise the matter directly with Tutoring4All management.
  2. Step 2: Tutoring4All will facilitate a resolution through liaison between parties.
  3. Step 3: Where a concern is serious, a formal investigation will be conducted.

8. Reporting Violations

Tutoring4All provides confidential mechanisms for reporting violations of this Code. Anonymous reporting options are available. All reports will be taken seriously and handled with discretion and fairness.

9. Consequences of Violations

Depending on the nature and severity of a breach, consequences may include a formal warning, suspension of tutoring arrangements, termination of the tutoring relationship, or referral to relevant legal authorities where required by law.

10. Review & Evaluation

This Code of Conduct is reviewed annually, with input and feedback considered from tutors, students, and families. All tutors are required to sign an acknowledgement that they have read, understood, and agreed to abide by this Code before commencing tutoring.

11. Legal Compliance

This Code of Conduct operates in alignment with the following legislation and frameworks:

  • NSW Anti-Discrimination Act 1977
  • Child Protection (Working with Children) Act 2012 (NSW)
  • Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)
  • NESA (NSW Education Standards Authority) guidelines
