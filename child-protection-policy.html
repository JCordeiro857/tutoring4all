Child Protection Policy

Tutoring4All is committed to the safety and wellbeing of every child in our care.

🚨

Emergency & Reporting Contacts

If a child is in immediate danger, always call 000 first. For child protection concerns in NSW:

🚒 Emergency: 000 📞 DCJ: 132 111 ✉️ Contact our Child Protection Officer

1. Introduction & Purpose

Tutoring4All is deeply committed to the safety, welfare, and wellbeing of all children and young people we work with. This policy reflects our commitment to exceeding child protection standards as required under the NSW Child Protection (Working with Children) Act 2012 and related legislation.

Our goal is to create and maintain safe environments for all students, ensure appropriate responses to any abuse or harm, and instil a culture where child safety is everyone's responsibility.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all Tutoring4All employees, tutors, volunteers, and contractors who work with or come into contact with children and young people under the age of 18 in the course of their role with Tutoring4All.

3. Definitions

  • Child: Any person under 18 years of age.
  • Child abuse: Includes physical abuse, emotional abuse, sexual abuse, and neglect.
  • WWCC: Working With Children Check — mandatory screening for all persons in child-facing roles in NSW.
  • Mandatory reporter: A person legally required to report suspected child abuse or neglect to relevant authorities.

4. Legal Framework

This policy operates in compliance with the following legislation and frameworks:

  • Child Protection (Working with Children) Act 2012 (NSW)
  • Children and Young Persons (Care and Protection) Act 1998 (NSW)
  • NESA guidelines for child safety in education settings
  • National Child Safe Standards
  • Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)

5. Core Obligations

  • All tutors must hold and maintain a current, valid Working With Children Check (WWCC) before commencing any tutoring role.
  • Any suspected child abuse or neglect must be reported immediately to the NSW Department of Communities and Justice (DCJ) or NSW Police where there is a risk to a child's safety.
  • Risk assessments must be considered when planning and conducting tutoring sessions.
  • All tutors must complete a review of this policy before commencing work with Tutoring4All.

6. Code of Conduct Requirements

In the context of child protection, all tutors must:

  • Treat all children and young people with respect and dignity at all times.
  • Maintain clear, appropriate professional boundaries — no intimate, romantic, or sexualised communication or contact of any kind.
  • Ensure the physical safety of students during every session.
  • Handle all student information with strict confidentiality.
  • Provide non-discriminatory, inclusive support regardless of background, disability, gender, or religion.
  • Never be alone with a child in a closed or private space without appropriate visibility.

7. Reporting Procedures

If a tutor, parent, or student has a concern about the safety or welfare of a child, the following steps should be followed:

  1. Immediate danger: Call 000 immediately.
  2. Reportable concerns: Notify the Tutoring4All Child Protection Officer at info@Tutoring4All.com.au or call 0490 423 713.
  3. DCJ: Contact the NSW Department of Communities and Justice on 132 111.
  4. Documentation: All concerns must be documented securely and confidentially as soon as practicable.

All reports are treated seriously, handled confidentially, and responded to promptly. Tutoring4All will cooperate fully with any investigation by NSW Police or DCJ.

8. Supervision Standards

One-on-one sessions: Should be conducted in visible, open areas where possible. A second adult's presence is encouraged where practicable. Sessions should not take place in enclosed, private spaces.

Group sessions: Must maintain appropriate adult-to-student ratios at all times.

Online sessions: Must use secure, approved platforms (Zoom, Teams). Screen recordings should not be made without explicit consent. Tutors must maintain professional boundaries in all online interactions.

9. Online Safety

  • Only approved, secure platforms are to be used for online tutoring.
  • Personal contact information must not be shared between tutors and students outside of Tutoring4All-sanctioned communication channels.
  • Social media connections between tutors and students are not permitted.
  • Any inappropriate online communication must be reported immediately.

10. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually, or sooner if there are changes to relevant legislation or serious incidents that require a policy update. All tutors are notified of any policy changes as they occur.
